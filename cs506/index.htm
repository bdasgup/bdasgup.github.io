  • The focus of this course is all basic and some advanced topics on quantum computing, i.e., computing models and related algorithmic developments of computers that use quantum mechanics. We will design efficient algorithms that can be run by quantum computers, contrast the computational powers of quantum computers with that of classical computational models, analyze algorithms written for quantum computers, and measure the efficiency of quantum algorithms. Apart from mathematical and quantum mechanics preliminaries, major topics will include computing framework of quantum mechanics, quantum computational models, quantum teleportation, and quantum algorithms such as Deutsch and Deutsch-Jozsa algorithms, Grover’s quantum search algorithm and Shor’s factoring algorithms.
  • Prerequisite:   CS 401 or equivalent
  • Tentative list of topics to be covered in random order (subject to change):
    1. Introduction to quantum behavior (experiments with bullets, waves and electrons, interference of electron waves, etc.)
    2. Basic concepts
      1. Relevant computing models
           (Turing machines, circuit models, probabilistic computation, reversible computation, etc.)
      2. Linear algebraic formulation for circuit models
      3. Beam splitter experiments
    3. Linear algebraic formalisms
      1. Dirac notation, Hilbert space, orthonormal basis, dual vectors, etc.
      2. Operators (identity, unitary, adjoint, functions of operators, etc.)
      3. Spectral linear algebra and spectral theorems
      4. Tensor products and Schmidt decomposition theorem
    4. Computing framework of quantum mechanics
      1. Qubits, quantum registers, composite systems, time evolution postulate
      2. Measurement postulates
      3. General quantum operations
      4. No-cloning theorem
    5. Quantum computational model (quantum gates, universality, measurement implementation)
    6. Quantum teleportation and some applications
    7. Some quantum algorithms
      1. Probabilistic vs. quantum computation
      2. Phase kick-back
      3. Deutsch and Deutsch-Jozsa algorithms
      4. Grover’s quantum search algorithm
      5. Shor’s factoring algorithms
    8. Additional topics such as (time permitting):
      1. Adiabatic computing
      2. Quantum information theory
      3. Quantum cryptography
      4. Quantum money
      5. .....
